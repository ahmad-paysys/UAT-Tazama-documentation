Purpose

Why it matters
UAT is the final validation gate before go-live. Its job is not to find technical bugs — that is the responsibility of QA. UAT answers one question: does this system do what the business actually needs it to do? It is conducted by business users, not developers. A system that passes every technical test can still fail UAT. Without formal sign-off, there is no accountability trail, no audit evidence, and no defensible basis for release.

Three-Week Execution Plan

Schedule
Week 1 Setup & Kick-off
Lock scope and out-of-scope list in writing — signed by client
Agree acceptance criteria per workflow before testing starts
Validate UAT environment mirrors production
Load realistic, production-volume test data
Brief client testers: they validate workflows, not technicalities
Week 2 Execution & Triage
Execute all test scenarios end-to-end in business language
Track pass / fail / blocked daily
Triage defects every day; critical ones resolved within the week
Assign disposition to every open defect: fix, defer, or accept
No undocumented "we'll fix it later" agreements
Week 3 Re-test & Sign-Off
Re-execute all failed and blocked scenarios after fixes
Assemble complete sign-off package (see deliverables)
Formally document any unexecuted tests and the reason
Hold sign-off meeting — not an email or Slack message
Obtain named signatures from named approvers

Required Deliverables

Sign-off package
Deliverable What it must contain When
Agreed Acceptance Criteria Given / When / Then format per workflow. Measurable and unambiguous — "loads in under 5 seconds," not "system is fast." Client-signed before Week 1 testing begins. Week 1
Scope & Out-of-Scope Doc Explicit list of features included and explicitly excluded. Prevents scope disputes and moving goalposts in Week 3. Week 1
Test Execution Report Scenarios executed, passed, failed, blocked. Daily progress tracking visible to client throughout Week 2. Week 2
Defect Disposition Log Every defect with severity, status, and agreed disposition. Deferred items must have a target sprint and date — not "next release." Week 2
Sign-Off Document Release version, environment tested, acceptance criteria pass/fail with evidence, testing summary, known risks, rollback plan, 72-hour monitoring plan. Named approvers with signatures. Week 3

Defect Severity & Disposition Policy

How to handle open issues at sign-off
Severity Definition Disposition at Sign-Off
Critical Blocks a core workflow; no viable workaround exists Must be fixed before sign-off. No exceptions.
Major Impacts a workflow but a documented workaround exists Conditional sign-off with explicit risk acceptance from client stakeholder and a committed fix date.
Minor Cosmetic or low-impact; does not affect business outcomes Deferred with a named sprint target. Recorded in disposition log.

Sign-Off Criteria

What "done" looks like
All acceptance criteria met

Every agreed Given / When / Then scenario passes, or has a documented, client-approved conditional disposition.

Zero open critical defects

No blocking issues remain unresolved. All criticals are closed or re-classified with stakeholder agreement.

Defect log complete

Every defect has a severity, status, and disposition. Nothing is in an undefined state.

Unexecuted tests declared

Any skipped test cases are explicitly listed with reasons. Client knowingly accepts the untested risk — it is documented.

Named approvers sign

Business owner, client representative, and QA lead each sign individually. Group approval or email acknowledgement is not sufficient.

Rollback plan confirmed

A tested rollback procedure is documented and agreed with operations before the sign-off meeting.

Managing a Difficult Client

Protective measures
Risks to Watch
  • Scope expansion in Week 3 — prevented by the signed scope document from Week 1
  • Vague refusal ("it's not ready") — counter with: which criterion failed, exactly?
  • Silence treated as acceptance — every disposition requires explicit written agreement
  • Deadline pressure to approve — conditional sign-off exists precisely for this situation
Non-Negotiable Rules
  • Never sign off before all scenarios are executed
  • Refused sign-off must cite specific failed criteria — not a general objection
  • Re-sign-off path must be defined in writing: which tests to re-run, by whom
  • "We'll hotfix in production" is not an acceptable resolution strategy
